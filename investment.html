Investment FAQs

What's the difference between saving and investing? +

Saving is setting aside money for future use, typically in safe, low-return instruments. Investing involves putting money into assets with the expectation of generating returns over time, which carries some risk but offers higher potential returns.

How do I determine my risk tolerance? +

Your risk tolerance depends on your financial goals, time horizon, and emotional comfort with market fluctuations. Consider factors like your age, income stability, financial obligations, and how you would react to potential investment losses.

What is diversification and why is it important? +

Diversification means spreading your investments across different asset classes, sectors, and geographies to reduce risk. It helps ensure that a decline in one investment doesn't significantly impact your overall portfolio.

How much should I invest each month? +

A common guideline is to invest at least 15-20% of your income. However, the ideal amount depends on your financial goals, current expenses, debt obligations, and emergency fund status. Start with what you can afford and gradually increase as your income grows.
